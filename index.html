inkonography, esther hong (2023)

marking the body is a sacred human practice that has existed since the dawn of the bronze-age. from the various gypsy tribes in india and the middle east, to the scythians in eastern europe, tattoos have been regarded as marks of distinction, belonging, and identification. like that of our ancestors, we paint symbols on our bodies to boldly make visible, in its permanence and pain, traces of who we are/what we cherish/who we wish to be. inkonography features a collection of photo clippings from various individuals and their tattoos as i've found them around the streets of durham and duke university. for this project, i seek to archive the markings of modern tattoos and the symbols that have been used to decorate, identify, and claim ownership of the self/body.
